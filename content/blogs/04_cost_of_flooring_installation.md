## The Myth of "Price Per Square Meter"

It is incredibly common for homeowners to walk into a big-box hardware store, see a pallet of laminate flooring marked "$15 per square meter," and mentally calculate that their 100sqm house will cost exactly $1,500 to floor. 

Unfortunately, this is a myth. 

The sticker price rarely includes the underlay, the matching scotia (edging), the transition trims for doorways, or the delivery fees. More importantly, it does not include the labor to tear up the old flooring, level the concrete slab beneath it, and expertly lay the new planks. 

When budgeting for your renovation, you must factor in the **Total Installed Cost**, not just the raw material cost.

## Breaking Down the Costs

Let’s break down the individual components that make up a professional flooring quote.

### 1. Material Costs
This is the price of the actual flooring product. Prices vary wildly depending on the quality, thickness, and material.
- **Entry-Level Laminate:** Very cheap, but highly susceptible to water damage and scratching.
- **Premium Hybrid Flooring:** Mid-to-high range. Offers exceptional value because it is 100% waterproof and highly durable.
- **Solid Hardwood:** The most expensive option, often double or triple the price of hybrid flooring.

### 2. Subfloor Preparation (The Hidden Cost)
This is the most common "surprise" cost in flooring renovations. Whether you are laying hybrid, laminate, or engineered timber, the subfloor (the concrete slab or wooden joists beneath) must be incredibly flat.

If your concrete slab is wavy or has divots, the rigid planks of modern flooring will bounce, creak, and eventually crack at the joints. 
- **Grinding:** If the slab has high spots, it must be ground down by professionals.
- **Leveling Compound:** If there are low spots, a self-leveling liquid compound must be poured to create a perfectly flat surface.

Subfloor leveling can add anywhere from a few hundred to over a thousand dollars to a project, depending on the severity of the unevenness. *Never skip this step.* A cheap installation on a bad subfloor will ruin expensive flooring.

### 3. Underlay Requirements
Underlay is a thin layer of foam or rubber placed between the subfloor and the floorboards. It provides acoustic dampening (so your floor doesn't sound hollow) and thermal insulation.
- **The Hybrid Advantage:** Premium hybrid flooring (like the ranges offered at Portland Flooring) often comes with a **pre-attached acoustic underlay**. This saves you the cost of buying separate rolls of underlay and saves the installer time (which saves you money on labor).

### 4. Installation Labor
Flooring installation is a skilled trade. While hybrid flooring utilizes a "click-lock" system that is faster to lay than traditional nail-down hardwood, it still requires precision cutting around door jambs, kitchen islands, and intricate corners.

In Victoria, installation rates are typically charged per square meter. The rate may increase if the layout is highly complex (e.g., laying on a diagonal pattern or dealing with many small, intricate rooms).

### 5. Accessories and Finishing Touches
A floor isn't finished until the edges are sealed. You will need to budget for:
- **Scotia or Skirting Boards:** To cover the mandatory expansion gap left around the perimeter of the room.
- **Transition Trims:** Metal or color-matched strips used in doorways where the new floor meets an existing floor (like carpet in a bedroom).
- **Stair Nosing:** If you are laying flooring on a staircase, specialized curved edge pieces are required.

## Cost Comparisons: Hybrid vs. Timber vs. Tiles

To give you an idea of overall value, let's look at how Hybrid flooring compares to other popular options.

- **Solid Timber:** Extremely expensive materials. Installation is slow, requiring glues, nails, sanding, and polishing on-site. Very high total installed cost.
- **Ceramic Tiles:** Moderate material cost. However, tiling labor is very expensive and time-consuming. You must also pay for grout, adhesives, and sealing.
- **Hybrid Flooring:** Premium material cost, but significantly lower installation labor costs compared to timber or tiles. No glues, no sanding, and no separate underlay required. **Offers the best balance of luxury aesthetics and overall installed value.**

## DIY vs. Professional Installation

Because hybrid and laminate floors use a click-lock system, many homeowners are tempted to take the DIY route to save on labor costs. 

**Is DIY worth it?**
If you are highly handy, have access to proper saws (table saw, drop saw, jigsaw), and understand how to check and level a subfloor, DIY is possible.

However, if you make a mistake—such as failing to leave the correct expansion gap around the walls—the entire floor can buckle as temperatures change. This voids the manufacturer's warranty. For peace of mind, a flawless finish around complex door frames, and protected warranties, we strongly recommend professional installation.

## How to Get an Accurate Quote in Victoria

To avoid nasty surprises halfway through your renovation, follow these steps to get an accurate quote:
1. **Get an In-Home Measure:** Do not rely on your own measurements. A professional will measure exactly, accounting for "wastage" (the offcuts that cannot be used).
2. **Request a Subfloor Inspection:** Ensure the quoting professional checks the level of your existing floor.
3. **Ask for an All-Inclusive Quote:** Ensure the quote clearly lists materials, installation, underlay (if not attached), trims, and subfloor prep estimates.
